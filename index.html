Why?

The world is burning. People are falling into endlessness by staring at screens. Avoiding responsibility has never been easier because of all the noise and the accuracy of algorithms in our smart devices. We tend to live in a virtual reality through a superficial life that leaves our brains screaming for authenticity and peace. It has never been easier to be distracted.

We face more mental health issues than ever before, and the trend is devastating. Suicide rates are climbing faster than Jack climbed the beanstalk. In theory people are more connected than ever, but we have never been further away from each other. The world is a noisy place to live in. The brain of the youth is in a developing stage, and if their support circle (parents etc.) are dopamine addicts, nobody has a chance of getting out of the loop.

People are neglecting the foundation of being humans that can function. Consistent sleep, exercise, and nutrition. When suffering from mental health issues, a minor inconvenience will feel like a mountain. It´s almost impossible to get back on your feet on your own and somehow still we as a humans are built the way that we don´t reach out for help soon enough or at all. It does not feel like an option.

We need structures, clarity, meaning, strength to talk.
It´s not easy to maintain focus as a curious individual. The world has been always full of color and adventures. It happens that when we grow up, we tend to take responsibility. That responsibility needs to be carried and it´s good to have people to look up to when you start to carry some weight for the very first times.

The world gets unfair. It feels toxic. But the fact is that the world is in fact the same colorful and adventurous place as it was when you were a child with no responsibilities. We just look at it through very different types of glasses, blurred by all the unfairness we face. In the end it´s our responsibility to provide the structure we would have needed ourselves.

Masses of people tend to do what is easy and what our environment leads us towards. Getting help to acquire the tools to reverse engineer your head to be even remotely okay has more friction than needed; the knowledge should be democratized, not gatekeeped. Even one minor traumatic experience that is not handled can cause multiple generations to get off the tracks. It also takes only one person in the bloodline to stop it, but the further it gets, it also gets harder.

Maintaining your attention span can be the factor whether you build something extremely meaningful or end up under a bridge – treat it with care. Apologize when you are in the wrong. Fight for things you consider are worth fighting for.
